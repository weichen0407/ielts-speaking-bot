# Vocabulary Suggestions

## Session: Collecting (NBA Jerseys)

### Words That Can Be Optimized

| Your Word | Better Alternative | Why |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| like (repeated) | enjoy, admire, have a passion for | Reduces repetition; sounds more natural |
| nice | appealing, satisfying, rewarding | More descriptive and precise |
| got some interest | developed an interest, sparked my curiosity | More natural phrasing |
| take photo | take pictures, snap photos, capture memories | More varied expression |
| wardrobe | collection, closet display, personal archive | More specific to collecting context |
| smart skills | basketball IQ, court vision, game intelligence | More natural collocation for sports context |
| amazed | in awe, captivated, fascinated | Stronger, more precise emotion words |
| got the champion title | won the championship, claimed the title | Correct collocation; "champion title" is non-standard |
| watch with her | watch with him | Pronoun agreement (grandpa → him) |

### Professional Vocabulary by Topic

#### Collecting / Hobbies
- **collection** (noun): a group of similar items gathered over time
  - Usage: "I've built up quite a collection over the years."
- **memorabilia** (noun): objects of sentimental or historical value
  - Usage: "I'm really into sports memorabilia, especially NBA jerseys."
- **acquire** (verb): to obtain or add to one's collection
  - Usage: "I try to acquire a new jersey every season."
- **display** (verb/noun): to arrange items for others to see
  - Usage: "I display my jerseys proudly in my room."
- **sentimental value** (phrase): emotional worth beyond monetary price
  - Usage: "Each jersey has sentimental value because it reminds me of a specific game."

#### Sports / NBA
- **enthusiast** (noun): a person who is very interested in a particular activity
  - Usage: "I'm a huge basketball enthusiast."
- **iconic** (adjective): widely recognized and well-established
  - Usage: "I collect jerseys of iconic players like Curry and LeBron."
- **dedicated fan** (phrase): a loyal and passionate supporter
  - Usage: "I've been a dedicated fan since I was a kid."
- **championship title** (phrase): the top prize in a sports league
  - Usage: "The Warriors claimed the championship title in 2022."
- **city edition jersey** (phrase): special NBA jerseys inspired by local culture
  - Usage: "My favorite is the 2022 city edition jersey featuring the Golden Gate Bridge."
- **three-point shooting** (phrase): shots made from beyond the arc
  - Usage: "Curry revolutionized three-point shooting in the NBA."
- **court vision** (noun): a player's ability to see and anticipate plays
  - Usage: "What sets Curry apart is his incredible court vision."

#### Family / Childhood Memories
- **bond over** (phrasal verb): to connect with someone through a shared interest
  - Usage: "My grandpa and I bonded over watching NBA games together."
- **pass down** (phrasal verb): to give or teach something to a younger generation
  - Usage: "My love for basketball was passed down from my grandpa."
- **fond memories** (phrase): pleasant recollections of the past
  - Usage: "I have fond memories of watching games at my grandpa's house."

### Grammar & Fluency Notes
- Avoid repeating "I like" / "I want" — vary with "I enjoy", "I'm keen on", "I'm really into"
- Use present perfect for ongoing hobbies: "I've been collecting since..." instead of "I like to collect"
- Use "so that I can..." instead of "so that I could you know" for clearer expression
- **Pronoun agreement**: When referring to a male (grandpa), use "him" not "her"
  - ❌ "I used to watch NBA games with her"
  - ✅ "I used to watch NBA games with him"
- **Articles with nouns**: Use "a/an/the" before singular countable nouns
  - ❌ "It's home Jersey" → ✅ "It's a home jersey"
  - ❌ "with various logo on the chest" → ✅ "with various logos on the chest"
- **"Champion title" vs "Championship title"**: The correct term is "championship title" or simply "championship"
  - ❌ "got the champion title" → ✅ "won the championship" / "claimed the title"
- **Adjective + noun order**: Use adjective before noun, not separated by comma
  - ❌ "His smart, skills" → ✅ "His smart skills" or "His intelligence and skills"
