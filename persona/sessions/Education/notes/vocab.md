# Vocabulary Suggestions

## Session: Education

### Words That Can Be Optimized

| Your Word | Better Alternative | Why |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| really keen | eager, enthusiastic, passionate about | "Really keen" is overused; "passionate about" sounds more committed and natural for IELTS |
| really great | highly promising, exceptionally rewarding, incredibly valuable | "Really great" is vague and informal; stronger adjectives show lexical range |
| currently listing | currently working in / currently involved in / currently pursuing | "Listing" is a misstep; "working in" or "involved in" is clearer and more accurate |
| influences (of the yoga industry) | influencer / thought leader / content creator | "Influences" (noun) means effects; "influencer" is the person — a common and important distinction |
| ways (to develop) | pathways / avenues / strategies | "Ways" is generic; "pathways" or "avenues" sounds more sophisticated and fluent |
| begin to get to know | was first introduced to / was exposed to | "Was first introduced to" is more precise and natural for describing how you discovered something |
| build front and interest | turn my interest / channel my passion | "Build front" is unclear; "channel my passion" is idiomatic and fluent |
| great tools | a valuable asset / a powerful combination | "Tools" is too literal here; "asset" or "combination" better conveys synergy of skills |
| lose some fat weights | lose weight / shed some pounds / get in shape | "Fat weights" is non-standard; "lose weight" or "get in shape" is natural and idiomatic |
| mental treatments | mental discipline / therapeutic practice / mindfulness | "Treatments" sounds clinical; "mindfulness" or "therapeutic practice" is more appropriate for yoga |
| soothe in yourself | soothe oneself / find inner calm / achieve tranquility | "Soothe in yourself" is grammatically incorrect; "find inner calm" is natural and expressive |
| intense me | intrigues me / challenges me / captivates me | "Intense me" is unclear; "intrigues me" or "captivates me" conveys genuine interest |

### Professional Vocabulary by Topic

#### Education & Career
- **field of study** (noun phrase): a particular area of academic specialization
  - Usage: "My field of study was communications and journalism."
- **pursue a career** (verb phrase): to work towards a professional goal
  - Usage: "I'm hoping to pursue a career that combines media and wellness."
- **niche** (noun): a specialized segment of a market or industry
  - Usage: "Yoga influencer marketing is quite a unique niche to explore."
- **mentor** (noun): an experienced person who guides and advises someone less experienced
  - Usage: "My mother has been my mentor throughout my yoga journey."
- **foundation** (noun): a basis or underlying support for something
  - Usage: "Years of consistent practice gave me a solid foundation in yoga."

#### Marketing & Media
- **affiliate marketing** (noun): performance-based marketing where a business rewards affiliates for traffic or sales
  - Usage: "I'm currently involved in affiliate marketing, which is a rapidly growing industry."
- **brand collaboration** (noun phrase): a partnership between a content creator and a company
  - Usage: "As a yoga influencer, I could explore brand collaborations with wellness companies."
- **content creation** (noun phrase): the process of producing material for digital platforms
  - Usage: "Content creation allows me to combine my communication skills with my passion for yoga."

#### Yoga & Wellness
- **mind-body connection** (noun phrase): the link between mental and physical states
  - Usage: "Yoga helped me truly understand the mind-body connection."
- **posture / asana** (noun): a specific body position in yoga practice
  - Usage: "I enjoy exploring challenging postures like headstands and arm balances."
- **mindfulness** (noun): the practice of being present and aware in the moment
  - Usage: "Yoga is not just physical exercise — it's a form of mindfulness."
- **holistic** (adjective): relating to the whole person — body, mind, and spirit
  - Usage: "Yoga offers a holistic approach to health and well-being."

### Useful Linking Phrases
- "To begin with, I studied..."
- "More specifically, I'm particularly drawn to..."
- "What really fascinates me is the idea of..."
- "Looking ahead, I see this as a promising pathway because..."
- "That being said, I also believe that..."
- "What first sparked my interest was..."
- "Over time, I came to realize that..."
- "Beyond the physical aspect, what truly resonates with me is..."

### Collocations to Remember
- **pursue** a career / a passion / an opportunity
- **combine** skills / interests / fields
- **explore** a niche / new avenues / possibilities
- **develop** a career / expertise / a personal brand
- **build** a following / a reputation / a portfolio
- **discover** a passion / a new perspective / inner peace
- **cultivate** a practice / mindfulness / discipline
- **draw** inspiration / a connection / a sense of calm

### Grammar & Expression Notes
- "Sing yogas" → Consider: "combining yoga with media" or "integrating yoga into my content"
- "The the industry" → Avoid repetition; use "this industry" or "the field"
- "Really great ways to develop" → Try: "a promising avenue for developing" or "an excellent opportunity to build"
- "Fascinating with" → Use "fascinated by" (correct preposition): "I've always been fascinated by yoga"
- "Sporty ive" → Likely meant "sporty" or "athletic" — "I've always been quite athletic"
- "Lose some fat weights of of my body" → Try: "lose some weight" or "shed a few pounds" (simpler and more natural)
- "Soothe in yourself" → Try: "soothe yourself" or "find a sense of inner peace"
- "Intense me" → Likely meant "intrigues me" — "the challenging postures really intrigue me"
