   - [-"It is a nice day."→] Changed to [+"It's a lovely day, isn't it?"+] — Adding a tag question ("isn't it?") makes the opener more conversational and engaging, inviting the listener to agree.

## Common Patterns to Avoid

1. **Using "gonna" instead of "going to"** — In IELTS speaking, avoid informal contractions like "gonna," "wanna," "gotta." Use full forms: "going to," "want to," "got to."

2. **Embedded question word order errors** — When a question is embedded inside another phrase (e.g., "Could you tell me..."), the word order changes from question order to statement order:
   - ❌ "Could you tell me where is the station?"
   - ✅ "Could you tell me where the station is?"

3. **Incorrect expressions for feelings** — "How's your feeling" is not idiomatic. Use:
   - "How are you feeling?" (current state)
   - "How do you feel about...?" (opinion)
   - "What's your feeling on...?" (opinion, formal)
